Selected Clinical Cases

Oncological Gastrectomy in a Super-Elderly Patient with Cardia Cancer

Specialty: Surgical Oncology · Procedure type: Laparoscopic-assisted gastrectomy with D2 dissection

An 81-year-old patient with localized adenocarcinoma of the gastric cardia and severe cardiopulmonary comorbidities was accepted for staged neoadjuvant strategy followed by minimally invasive gastrectomy. Key focus was intraoperative hemodynamic control, pancreaticojejunal anastomosis safety and early nutrition.

Outcome: R0 resection, no Clavien-Dindo ≥ III complication, discharged on day 9, resumed oral chemotherapy at week 5. This case illustrates oncological radicality combined with geriatric surgical optimization.

Acute Perforated Diverticulitis with Faecal Peritonitis

Specialty: Emergency Surgery · Procedure type: Laparoscopic peritoneal lavage + Hartmann’s option

A 62-year-old patient presented with Hinchey III disease and septic shock on admission. Operative strategy prioritized source control in the shortest safe time, biomarkers-guided resuscitation and planned re-look policy in high-risk patients.

Outcome: Source control achieved within 55 minutes from incision; ICU stay 2 days; ward stay 7 days; no anastomotic leak. Illustrates the principle: damage control first, colostomy second, continuity third.

Synchronous Liver and Colon Metastases: Two-Stage Strategy

Specialty: Oncological Surgery · Procedure type: Laparoscopic colectomy followed by staged hepatic resection

Synchronous colorectal liver metastases in a 54-year-old patient were managed with a two-stage curative-intent pathway: minimally invasive colectomy first, then laparoscopic liver resection after recovery and multidisciplinary review.

Outcome: R0 in both sites, no breakthrough complications, adjuvant chemotherapy completed. Demonstrates sequencing reasoning in oligometastatic abdominal oncology.

Complicated Gallstone Disease in a Patient with Severe Cirrhosis

Specialty: Emergency + Complex MIS · Procedure type: Laparoscopic cholecystectomy with adhesive management

A patient with Child-Pugh B cirrhosis and acute cholecystitis required surgery despite high bleeding risk. The case highlights trocar/energy selection, low-pressure pneumoperitoneum, topical hemostasis and postoperative variceal/coagulation monitoring.

Outcome: Completed laparoscopically, no bleeding event, no hepatic decompensation, discharged day 5. Shows that high-risk patients are not automatically “open-only” if the surgical plan is case-tailored.
